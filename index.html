10 Fun Facts About Arizona

  1. Grand Canyon State: Arizona is home to the Grand Canyon National Park, one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors annually.
  2. State Flower: The state flower is the saguaro cactus blossom. The saguaro cactus, also native to Arizona, can live for 150 to 200 years.
  3. Meteor Crater: Arizona has the best-preserved meteorite impact site on Earth, located near Winslow.
  4. Sunshine: Arizona is one of the sunniest states in the U.S., with Phoenix experiencing sunshine for about 85% of its daylight hours.
  5. Population Diversity: It has a significant Native American population, with 22 sovereign American Indian communities recognized within the state.
  6. London Bridge: The original London Bridge was transported stone-by-stone and reconstructed in Lake Havasu City, Arizona.
  7. Copper State: Arizona produces more copper than any other state in the U.S., earning it the nickname, "The Copper State."
  8. State Reptile: The state reptile is the Arizona ridge-nosed rattlesnake. It was adopted as the state reptile in 1986.
  9. Gemstones: Arizona is the only state in the U.S. where all four precious gemstones (sapphire, ruby, emerald, and diamond) have been found.
  10. Old West History: The town of Tombstone, famous for the Gunfight at the O.K. Corral, is located in Arizona. It's a popular tourist destination for those interested in the Old West.
